Definitions

Welcome to the Definitions page of our safeguarding hub. Here, we provide clear and concise definitions of key terms related to safeguarding. Understanding these terms is essential for anyone involved in safeguarding practices, whether you're a professional, volunteer, or simply interested in learning more about this important field.

Empowerment

Definition:

Supporting individuals to have control and make informed choices about their own lives.

Application:

This means raising awareness, providing information, offering advice, and advocating for individuals when necessary to help them understand their rights and make choices about their safety and well-being.

Example:

A person in a care home should be supported to make decisions about their own care, rather than having decisions made for them.

Prevention

Definition:

Taking proactive measures to identify and address potential risks and concerns before harm occurs.

Application:

This involves implementing measures to reduce the risk of abuse and neglect, such as conducting risk assessments and raising awareness about safeguarding.

Example:

Implementing safety measures like non-slip floors and handrails in residential care settings to prevent accidents.

Proportionality

Definition:

Ensuring that actions taken are proportionate to the level of risk involved, meaning the response should be appropriate and not overly intrusive.

Application:

This involves balancing the duty to safeguard with the individual's rights and privacy.

Example:

In cases of suspected abuse or neglect, conducting a thorough investigation while respecting the person's dignity and privacy.

Protection

Definition:

Ensuring individuals are safe from harm, abuse, neglect, and exploitation.

Application:

Protection involves creating environments and systems that safeguard vulnerable individuals, identifying risks, and taking proactive measures to prevent harm. This includes intervention when necessary, as well as policies and practices that support safety and well-being.

Example:

A social worker ensures that a child experiencing neglect receives appropriate care and protection by working with authorities and support services to remove them from a harmful situation.

Partnership

Definition:

Working together with individuals, organisations, and communities to promote safety and well-being.

Application:

Effective safeguarding relies on collaboration between professionals, families, and support networks. Partnership involves sharing knowledge, coordinating efforts, and fostering trust to ensure individuals receive the best possible protection and care. This includes multi-agency working to address risks and provide holistic support.

Example:

A local authority, healthcare provider, and community organization work together to support an adult at risk by sharing relevant information and coordinating services to ensure their safety and independence.

Accountability

Definition:

Ensuring transparency, responsibility, and ownership in safeguarding practices to uphold safety and well-being.

Application:

Accountability involves individuals and organizations taking responsibility for their actions and decisions in safeguarding. It includes setting clear policies, monitoring effectiveness, learning from mistakes, and ensuring that concerns are addressed appropriately. This principle reinforces trust and integrity within safeguarding frameworks.

Example:

A care provider conducts regular audits and reviews safeguarding procedures to ensure that staff follow best practices, report concerns effectively, and continuously improve their approach to protecting vulnerable individuals.
